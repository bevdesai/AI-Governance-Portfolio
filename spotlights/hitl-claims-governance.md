# Project Spotlight: Governance Framework for AI-Augmented Claims Processing
Role: AI Enablement & Governance Leader

Domain: P&C Insurance (Claims)
# Situation
The organization sought to implement an AI-driven "fast-track" for low-complexity property claims. While the goal was to reduce settlement time, the primary risk was "Algorithm Bias" or "Automatic Approval" of fraudulent or over-scoped claims without professional oversight.
# Task
I was tasked with designing the Governance Guardrails that would allow for automation while ensuring a licensed adjuster remained the final decision-maker for edge cases and high-dollar settlements.
# Action
  - Threshold-Based Routing: Established a strict "Risk Scoring" engine. Any claim with a confidence score below 85% or a value exceeding a specific $USD$ threshold was automatically diverted to a human queue.
  - Explainability Requirements: Mandated that the AI tool provide "Reasoning Tags" (e.g., "Flagging for potential roof age discrepancy") so the human adjuster could quickly validate the AI's logic rather than blindly trusting the output.
  - Feedback Loop Integration: Implemented a "Quality Audit" workflow where human corrections were fed back into the model retraining pipeline to improve future accuracy and reduce "False Positives.
# Result
 - Efficiency: Achieved a 30% reduction in average claim cycle time for low-risk profiles.
 - Compliance: Maintained 100% adherence to state-level insurance "fair handling" regulations by ensuring a human "Review & Sign-off" for all final settlements.
  - Operational Trust: Successfully onboarded the claims adjusting team by positioning the AI as a "Co-pilot" rather than a replacement.
